# ASCENT: Sacituzumab Govitecan in Metastatic TNBC

## Trial Identity
- **Full name:** ASCENT (IMMU-132-05)
- **Phase:** III, open-label, randomized
- **Population:** Relapsed or refractory metastatic TNBC, ≥2 prior therapies
- **Primary endpoint:** Progression-free survival (PFS) in patients without brain metastases
- **Key publication:** Bardia A et al., NEJM 2021
- **Sponsor:** Gilead Sciences / Immunomedics

## Drug Profile: Sacituzumab Govitecan (Trodelvy)
- **Class:** Antibody-drug conjugate (ADC)
- **Target:** Trop-2 (trophoblast cell-surface antigen 2) — highly expressed in TNBC
- **Payload:** SN-38 (active metabolite of irinotecan, topoisomerase I inhibitor)
- **Mechanism:** Trop-2-directed binding → internalization → SN-38 release → DNA damage → apoptosis
- **Drug-antibody ratio:** ~7.6 (high payload, bystander effect)

## Key Efficacy Results

### Progression-Free Survival (primary endpoint, brain met-free population)
| Metric | Sacituzumab | Chemotherapy |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Median PFS | 5.6 months | 1.7 months |
| HR | 0.41 (95% CI: 0.32–0.52) | — |
| p-value | <0.001 | — |

### Overall Survival
| Metric | Sacituzumab | Chemotherapy |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Median OS | 12.1 months | 6.7 months |
| HR | 0.48 (95% CI: 0.38–0.59) | — |
| p-value | <0.001 | — |

### Objective Response Rate (ORR)
- ORR: 35% (sacituzumab) vs 5% (chemotherapy)
- CR: 4% vs <1%
- Median duration of response: 6.3 months vs 3.6 months

## Biomarker Analysis

### Trop-2 Expression
- Benefit observed regardless of Trop-2 expression level (H-score)
- No validated cut-off for patient selection (unlike HER2 or PD-L1)
- High Trop-2: most pronounced benefit (exploratory)

### BRCA Status
- BRCA1/2 mutation: PFS HR 0.43 (similar to overall)
- BRCA wild-type: PFS HR 0.40
- Benefit independent of BRCA status

### Prior Therapy
- Benefit seen regardless of prior checkpoint inhibitor use
- Patients who progressed on pembrolizumab: still benefited from sacituzumab

## Safety
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: 54% (sacituzumab) vs 35% (chemotherapy)
- Most common Grade ≥3 AEs:
  - Neutropenia: 51% (sacituzumab) — growth factor support often required
  - Leukopenia: 10%
  - Diarrhea: 10%
  - Anemia: 8%
- UGT1A1*28 homozygous patients: increased SN-38 exposure → higher toxicity risk (dose reduction may be needed)
- Alopecia: 46% (Grade 1–2)

## Key Eligibility for ASCENT
- Metastatic TNBC (confirmed ER <1%, PR <1%, HER2-negative)
- ≥2 prior chemotherapy regimens (≥1 for metastatic disease)
- ECOG PS 0–1
- No active CNS metastases (brain met subgroup analyzed separately)

## UGT1A1 Testing
- UGT1A1*28 genotyping: identifies poor metabolizers of SN-38
- Homozygous *28/*28: consider starting at 7.5 mg/kg (vs standard 10 mg/kg)
- Not a hard contraindication but informs toxicity management

## Clinical Implications for Helix
- **Setting:** 3rd line+ metastatic TNBC (after anthracycline, taxane, and prior therapies)
- **No biomarker gate:** No companion diagnostic required for current indication
- **Sequencing:** Can follow pembrolizumab-based regimens; benefit is independent of prior checkpoint inhibitor
- **BRCA+ patients:** Sacituzumab and olaparib are both options in metastatic setting; no direct comparison available
- **Trop-2:** High expression correlates with response but is not required for treatment decision

## Regulatory Status
- FDA approved: April 2021 (unresectable locally advanced or metastatic TNBC, ≥2 prior therapies)
- Extended approval: February 2023 (HR+ HER2-negative, ≥2 prior endocrine regimens)
- EMA approved: November 2021
