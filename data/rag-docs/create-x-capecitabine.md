# CREATE-X: Adjuvant Capecitabine in TNBC with Residual Disease

## Trial Identity
- **Full name:** CREATE-X (JBCRG-04)
- **Phase:** III, open-label, randomized
- **Population:** HER2-negative breast cancer with residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant chemotherapy
- **Primary endpoint:** Disease-free survival (DFS)
- **Key publication:** Masuda N et al., NEJM 2017
- **Sponsor:** Chugai Pharmaceutical (Japan); cooperative group trial

## Clinical Context
CREATE-X addresses a critical unmet need: patients with TNBC who do NOT achieve pathological complete response (pCR) after neoadjuvant chemotherapy have substantially worse prognosis. This trial established capecitabine as standard adjuvant therapy in this high-risk residual disease population.

## Intervention
- **Experimental arm:** Capecitabine 1250 mg/m² PO twice daily, days 1–14 of 21-day cycle × 8 cycles
- **Control arm:** Observation (no additional therapy)
- **Timing:** After surgery, following non-pCR to neoadjuvant anthracycline ± taxane

## Key Efficacy Results

### Disease-Free Survival (DFS) — Primary Endpoint
| Metric | Capecitabine | Observation |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| 5-year DFS | 74.1% | 67.6% |
| HR | 0.70 (95% CI: 0.53–0.92) | — |
| p-value | 0.01 | — |

### Overall Survival (OS) — Key Secondary
| Metric | Capecitabine | Observation |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| 5-year OS | 89.2% | 83.9% |
| HR | 0.59 (95% CI: 0.39–0.90) | — |
| p-value | 0.01 | — |

## TNBC Subgroup (Key for Helix)

### DFS in TNBC Patients
- 5-year DFS: 69.8% (capecitabine) vs 56.1% (observation)
- HR: 0.58 (95% CI: 0.39–0.87)
- **Absolute DFS benefit: +13.7%** — substantially larger than overall population

### OS in TNBC Patients
- 5-year OS: 78.8% vs 70.3%
- HR: 0.52 (95% CI: 0.30–0.90)
- **Absolute OS benefit: +8.5%** in TNBC

The TNBC subgroup showed numerically greater benefit than the HR+ subgroup, making this especially relevant for Helix's TNBC population.

## Safety
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: 73.4% (capecitabine) vs 23.5% (observation) — largely driven by hand-foot syndrome
- Common AEs (capecitabine):
  - Hand-foot syndrome (palmar-plantar erythrodysesthesia): 73.4% any grade; 10.0% Grade ≥3
  - Nausea: 36.1%
  - Diarrhea: 35.7%
  - Neutropenia: 16.9% Grade ≥3
  - Stomatitis: 30.4%
- Dose reductions required in ~40% of patients

## Practical Dosing Considerations
- Japanese population study — Western patients may require dose adjustments
- Standard Western dose often used: 1000 mg/m² BID (reduced from 1250 mg/m²) to improve tolerability
- DPD deficiency testing: Consider before starting (risk of severe toxicity)

## Clinical Implications for Helix

### Who benefits most
- TNBC patients with **residual invasive disease** after neoadjuvant chemo (non-pCR)
- This is the single most important eligibility criterion
- pCR patients: No evidence of capecitabine benefit (not studied in CREATE-X for pCR patients)

### Integration with pembrolizumab era
- CREATE-X predates KEYNOTE-522
- In pembrolizumab-treated patients with residual disease: capecitabine may still be used, but evidence base is CREATE-X (no pembrolizumab overlap)
- Post-KEYNOTE-522 residual disease: adjuvant pembrolizumab continuation takes precedence; capecitabine role is being studied

### Sequencing guidance
1. Neoadjuvant pembrolizumab + chemo (KEYNOTE-522)
2. Surgery
3. If pCR → adjuvant pembrolizumab continuation (recommended)
4. If residual disease → capecitabine ± adjuvant pembrolizumab (evidence evolving)

## Regulatory Status
- Incorporated into NCCN guidelines: Category 1 recommendation for TNBC with residual disease
- ESMO guidelines: Recommended in non-pCR TNBC
- Not formally FDA-approved specifically for this indication (capecitabine is approved for MBC)
