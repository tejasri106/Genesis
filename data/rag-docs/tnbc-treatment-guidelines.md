# TNBC Treatment Guidelines: NCCN & ESMO Reference

## Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (TNBC) Definition

- ER (estrogen receptor): <1% positive cells by IHC
- PR (progesterone receptor): <1% positive cells by IHC
- HER2: IHC 0 or 1+; or FISH non-amplified (ratio <2.0)
- Confirmed on core biopsy or surgical specimen
- ~15–20% of all breast cancers; disproportionately affects younger women and women of African descent

---

## Early-Stage TNBC Treatment Algorithm

### Stage I (T1, N0)
- **pT1a (≤5mm):** Surgery → observation (low risk; adjuvant chemotherapy controversial)
- **pT1b (6–10mm):** Surgery → consider adjuvant chemotherapy (AC × 4 or TC × 4)
- **pT1c (11–20mm):** Neoadjuvant or adjuvant chemotherapy strongly recommended

### Stage II–III (T2+ or N+) — Standard of Care 2024

#### Neoadjuvant (Preferred)
1. **Pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w + carboplatin/paclitaxel** (12 weeks, weekly taxane preferred)
   - KEYNOTE-522 regimen — Category 1 NCCN recommendation
   - Then: AC × 4 cycles
   - Surgery
   - Then: Adjuvant pembrolizumab × 9 cycles

2. **AC-T (Anthracycline-Cyclophosphamide → Taxane)** — if pembrolizumab not eligible/available
   - AC × 4 → paclitaxel weekly × 12 or docetaxel q3w × 4
   - pCR rates: ~30–45% (without pembrolizumab)

#### Post-Neoadjuvant (Based on pCR Status)
| pCR Status | Recommended Adjuvant Therapy |
|------------|------------------------------|
| **pCR achieved** | Continue adjuvant pembrolizumab (up to 9 cycles, completing KEYNOTE-522 protocol) |
| **Residual disease** | Capecitabine × 8 cycles (CREATE-X; Category 1) ± consider pembrolizumab completion |
| BRCA+ with residual disease | Olaparib × 1 year (OlympiA; Category 1) — can follow capecitabine or concurrent (evolving) |

### Adjuvant-Only Approach (if neoadjuvant not pursued)
- Stage I–II: AC × 4 → paclitaxel q2w × 4 (dose-dense) — preferred
- BRCA+ patients: Olaparib adjuvant after completing standard chemo (OlympiA data)

## Metastatic TNBC Treatment Algorithm

### 1st Line (No Prior Chemotherapy for Metastatic Disease)

| Biomarker | Preferred Regimen |
|-----------|------------------|
| PD-L1 CPS ≥10 | Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (KEYNOTE-355) |
| PD-L1 CPS <10 | Chemotherapy alone (taxane, gemcitabine/carboplatin, capecitabine) |
| BRCA1/2 mutated | Olaparib or talazoparib (if PD-L1 <10 or pembrolizumab not suitable) |

**KEYNOTE-355 (1st line metastatic):**
- Pembrolizumab + nab-paclitaxel or paclitaxel or gemcitabine/carboplatin
- PFS benefit in PD-L1 CPS ≥10: HR 0.65

### 2nd Line
- Sacituzumab govitecan (ASCENT) — Category 1 after ≥2 prior therapies
- Platinum-based therapy (carboplatin) if BRCA+ and not prior platinum
- Eribulin, capecitabine, gemcitabine (standard cytotoxic options)

### 3rd Line+
- Sacituzumab govitecan if not used in 2nd line
- Clinical trial enrollment strongly recommended
- PARP inhibitors if BRCA+ and not previously used

## BRCA Testing Recommendations

- **Who to test:** All TNBC patients (germline BRCA testing recommended regardless of family history — NCCN 2023)
- **When:** At diagnosis or at metastatic diagnosis if not previously done
- **Test type:** Germline (blood/saliva) — required for olaparib/talazoparib approval
- **Referral:** Genetic counseling recommended for all BRCA mutation carriers

---

## PD-L1 Testing Recommendations

| Setting | Testing |
|---------|---------|
| Early TNBC (stage II–III) | Not required for pembrolizumab use (benefit regardless of PD-L1 in KEYNOTE-522) |
| Metastatic 1st line | Required — CPS with 22C3 pharmDx for pembrolizumab eligibility |
| Metastatic 2nd line+ | Not required for sacituzumab |

---

## Germline Testing Beyond BRCA

- **PALB2:** Emerging PARP inhibitor sensitivity data; referral to genetics
- **ATM, CHEK2, RAD51:** HRD without BRCA; platinum sensitivity possible
- **NTRK fusion:** Larotrectinib/entrectinib if NTRK fusion-positive (rare in TNBC)
- **MSI-H/dMMR:** Pembrolizumab approved for any MSI-H solid tumor
- **TMB ≥10 mut/Mb:** Pembrolizumab may be considered (FDA tumor-agnostic)

## Special Populations

### Inflammatory Breast Cancer (IBC)
- Neoadjuvant chemotherapy mandatory before surgery
- Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy if stage III IBC TNBC
- Mastectomy + radiation required (breast-conserving surgery contraindicated)

### TNBC in Pregnancy
- Chemotherapy may be given after 1st trimester (2nd–3rd trimester)
- Pembrolizumab: Contraindicated in pregnancy (immune-mediated fetal harm)
- Deliver early if possible to complete treatment

### Older Adults (≥65 years)
- Geriatric assessment recommended before intensive neoadjuvant regimens
- Dose-dense AC-T may be modified
- Pembrolizumab: No age-specific dose adjustment; monitor immune AEs

---

## Radiation Therapy Guidelines

- **Post-mastectomy:** Recommended for T3/T4 or ≥4 positive lymph nodes; consider for 1–3 positive nodes
- **Lumpectomy:** Whole-breast radiation mandatory for all stages
- **Regional nodal irradiation:** Per nodal involvement — SLN positive after neoadjuvant
- **Timing:** After completion of adjuvant chemotherapy

---

## Surveillance After Treatment

| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| History and physical | Every 3–6 months × 3 years, then annually |
| Mammogram (if breast preserved) | Annually |
| CA 15-3 (optional) | Not routinely recommended; consider if rising trend noted clinically |
| Imaging (CT/PET) | Only for specific symptoms; not routine surveillance |
| BRCA mutation carriers | Annual breast MRI + mammogram; gynecologic surveillance |
