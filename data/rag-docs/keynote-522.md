# KEYNOTE-522: Pembrolizumab + Chemotherapy in Early TNBC

## Trial Identity
- **Full name:** KEYNOTE-522
- **Phase:** III, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Population:** Previously untreated stage II–III triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC)
- **Primary endpoints:** Pathological complete response (pCR) and event-free survival (EFS)
- **Key publication:** Schmid P et al., NEJM 2022; NEJM 2020 (interim)
- **Sponsor:** Merck (MSD)

## Intervention
- **Experimental arm:** Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV q3w + carboplatin/paclitaxel (neoadjuvant) → pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w (adjuvant, up to 9 cycles)
- **Control arm:** Placebo + carboplatin/paclitaxel (neoadjuvant) → placebo (adjuvant)
- **Surgery:** Performed after neoadjuvant phase

## Key Efficacy Results

### Pathological Complete Response (pCR)
| Group | pCR Rate |
|-------|----------|
| Pembrolizumab + chemo | 64.8% |
| Placebo + chemo | 51.2% |
| **Absolute difference** | **+13.6%** (95% CI: 5.4–21.8) |

- pCR defined as ypT0/Tis ypN0 at surgery

### Event-Free Survival (EFS)
- Hazard ratio (HR): 0.63 (95% CI: 0.48–0.82)
- 36-month EFS: 84.5% (pembrolizumab) vs 76.8% (placebo)
- Benefit observed regardless of PD-L1 status

### Overall Survival (OS) — 5-year update
- HR for OS: 0.66 (95% CI: 0.50–0.87)
- 5-year OS: 86.6% vs 81.7%

## Biomarker Subgroups

### PD-L1 Expression
- PD-L1 CPS ≥1: pCR benefit most pronounced
- PD-L1 CPS <1: pCR benefit still present (~8% absolute)
- EFS benefit consistent across PD-L1 subgroups

### BRCA Status
- Exploratory analysis: pCR benefit observed in both BRCA-mutated and BRCA wild-type patients
- BRCA1/2 mutation did not diminish pembrolizumab benefit

## Safety
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: 78.0% (pembrolizumab) vs 72.8% (placebo)
- Immune-related AEs: 34.0% (pembrolizumab) vs 18.0% (placebo)
- Common irAEs: hypothyroidism, pneumonitis, adrenal insufficiency
- Treatment discontinuation due to AEs: 23.9% vs 12.4%

## Eligibility Criteria (Key)
- Stage II–III TNBC (confirmed ER/PR <1%, HER2-negative)
- No prior systemic therapy for TNBC
- ECOG PS 0–1
- No active autoimmune disease
- No prior immunotherapy

## Clinical Implications for Helix
- **Standard of care** recommendation: Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy for stage II–III TNBC (neoadjuvant)
- PD-L1 testing: Not required for eligibility (benefit seen regardless), but CPS ≥10 shows strongest benefit
- BRCA testing: Does not change pembrolizumab eligibility but informs PARP inhibitor consideration
- Post-surgery: Continue adjuvant pembrolizumab regardless of pCR status (EFS benefit maintained)

## Regulatory Status
- FDA approved: July 2021 (neoadjuvant + adjuvant high-risk early TNBC)
- EMA approved: 2022
