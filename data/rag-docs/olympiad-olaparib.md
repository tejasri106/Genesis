# OlympiAD: Olaparib (PARP Inhibitor) in BRCA-Mutated Breast Cancer

## Trial Identity
- **Full name:** OlympiAD
- **Phase:** III, open-label, randomized
- **Population:** HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer with germline BRCA1/2 mutation
- **Primary endpoint:** Progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Key publication:** Robson M et al., NEJM 2017; Robson M et al., Annals of Oncology 2019 (OS update)
- **Sponsor:** AstraZeneca

## Intervention
- **Experimental arm:** Olaparib 300 mg PO twice daily (monotherapy)
- **Control arm:** Physician's choice chemotherapy (capecitabine, eribulin, or vinorelbine)
- **Prior lines:** ≤2 prior chemotherapy lines for metastatic disease; prior platinum allowed if >12 months before enrollment

## Key Efficacy Results

### Progression-Free Survival (PFS)
| Metric | Olaparib | Chemotherapy |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Median PFS | 7.0 months | 4.2 months |
| HR | 0.58 (95% CI: 0.43–0.80) | — |
| p-value | <0.001 | — |

### Overall Survival (OS) — final analysis
- Median OS: 19.3 months (olaparib) vs 17.1 months (chemotherapy)
- HR: 0.90 (95% CI: 0.66–1.23) — not statistically significant
- Pre-planned subgroup: patients without prior chemotherapy for metastatic disease: HR 0.57

### Objective Response Rate (ORR)
- ORR: 59.9% (olaparib) vs 28.8% (chemotherapy)
- CR rate: 9.0% vs 1.5%

## BRCA Subgroup Analysis

### BRCA1 vs BRCA2
| BRCA Status | HR (PFS) |
|-------------|----------|
| BRCA1 mutation | 0.57 |
| BRCA2 mutation | 0.62 |
- Benefit consistent across BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutation carriers

### TNBC Subgroup
- TNBC patients with BRCA mutation: PFS HR 0.55
- TNBC benefit directionally stronger than HR+ subgroup

## Safety
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: 36.6% (olaparib) vs 50.5% (chemotherapy)
- Common AEs (olaparib): nausea (57.8%), anemia (40.3%), fatigue (36.7%), vomiting (29.6%)
- Grade ≥3 anemia: 16.1% (olaparib) — monitor CBC
- Serious AEs: 20.1% vs 20.9% (comparable)

## Eligibility Criteria
- Germline BRCA1/2 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variant (confirmed by validated test)
- HER2-negative (ER+/HER2- or TNBC)
- Metastatic disease
- ≤2 prior chemotherapy regimens for metastatic disease
- ECOG PS 0–2

## Companion Diagnostic
- BRACAnalysis CDx (Myriad Genetics) — FDA-approved companion diagnostic
- Germline testing required (somatic BRCA not sufficient for this indication)

## Clinical Implications for Helix
- **Indication:** BRCA1/2-mutated HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer
- **TNBC relevance:** Strong benefit signal in BRCA+ TNBC patients
- **Eligibility flag:** If patient has BRCA1 or BRCA2 mutation → olaparib should be flagged as eligible option
- **BRCA wild-type:** No evidence of benefit — do not recommend PARP inhibition
- **Sequence:** Consider after platinum-based therapy in metastatic setting; no direct comparison with pembrolizumab in early TNBC

## Related Trials
- OlympiA: Olaparib adjuvant in early BRCA+ HER2-negative breast cancer (pCR or residual disease) — HR for iDFS: 0.58
- BROCADE3: Veliparib + chemotherapy in BRCA+ metastatic breast cancer

## Regulatory Status
- FDA approved: January 2018 (BRCA+ HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer)
- Extended approval (OlympiA data): August 2022 (adjuvant, high-risk early BRCA+ breast cancer)
