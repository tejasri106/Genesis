# TNBC Staging, Pathology, and Molecular Subtypes

## TNM Staging (AJCC 8th Edition)

### T — Primary Tumor
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| T1a | >1mm but ≤5mm |
| T1b | >5mm but ≤10mm |
| T1c | >10mm but ≤20mm |
| T2 | >20mm but ≤50mm |
| T3 | >50mm |
| T4a | Extension to chest wall |
| T4b | Skin involvement (peau d'orange, ulceration) |
| T4d | Inflammatory breast cancer |

### N — Regional Lymph Nodes (Clinical)
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| N0 | No regional lymph node involvement |
| N1 | Movable ipsilateral axillary nodes (level I–II) |
| N2a | Fixed/matted ipsilateral axillary nodes |
| N2b | Clinically apparent internal mammary nodes |
| N3 | Ipsilateral infraclavicular, internal mammary + axillary, or supraclavicular |

### Pathological Staging After Neoadjuvant (ypTNM)
- **ypT0 ypN0:** Complete pathological response (pCR) — best prognosis
- **ypTis ypN0:** pCR (in situ residual only — equivalent to pCR by KEYNOTE-522 definition)
- **ypT+ and/or ypN+:** Residual invasive disease — warrants capecitabine (CREATE-X) ± continued pembrolizumab

### Stage Grouping
| Overall Stage | T | N | M | 5-yr OS (TNBC, approx.) |
|---------------|---|---|---|------------------------|
| Stage I | T1 | N0 | M0 | ~90% |
| Stage IIA | T2 N0 or T1 N1 | — | M0 | ~80% |
| Stage IIB | T2 N1 or T3 N0 | — | M0 | ~70% |
| Stage IIIA | T3 N1, T0–3 N2 | — | M0 | ~55% |
| Stage IIIB | T4 any N | — | M0 | ~45% |
| Stage IIIC | any T N3 | — | M0 | ~40% |
| Stage IV | any T any N M1 | — | — | ~20–30% (2-yr) |

## Pathological Complete Response (pCR)

### Definition
- **pCR (Helix/KEYNOTE-522):** ypT0/Tis ypN0 — no invasive cancer in breast OR lymph nodes
- Residual DCIS (ductal carcinoma in situ) alone: acceptable as pCR in some definitions
- Residual invasive: any residual invasive cancer = non-pCR regardless of size

### pCR Rates by Regimen
| Regimen | pCR Rate |
|---------|----------|
| AC-T (standard) | ~30–35% |
| AC-T + carboplatin | ~40–45% |
| Pembrolizumab + chemo (KEYNOTE-522) | 64.8% |
| Olaparib + chemo (BRCA+) | Limited data |

### pCR as Surrogate
- pCR correlates with EFS and OS in TNBC more strongly than HR+ disease
- Non-pCR TNBC: 5-year DFS ~56% → capecitabine improves to ~70% (CREATE-X)

## Molecular Subtypes of TNBC (Vanderbilt Classification)

### 1. BL1 (Basal-Like 1)
- Profile: High Ki-67, high cell cycle gene expression, DNA damage response activation
- Frequency: ~25%
- Treatment sensitivity: Highest pCR rates with chemotherapy; BRCA pathway activation
- Key drugs: Platinum agents, PARP inhibitors, pembrolizumab

### 2. BL2 (Basal-Like 2)
- Profile: Growth factor signaling (EGFR, MET), glycolysis genes
- Frequency: ~15%
- Treatment sensitivity: Lower pCR; more chemoresistant
- Key drugs: EGFR inhibitors (investigational), anti-angiogenics

### 3. M (Mesenchymal)
- Profile: Epithelial-mesenchymal transition, stem cell features, low proliferation
- Frequency: ~20%
- Treatment sensitivity: PI3K/AKT pathway; olaparib if HRD
- Key drugs: AKT inhibitors (capivasertib — CAPITELLO-291), mTOR inhibitors

### 4. MSL (Mesenchymal Stem-Like)
- Profile: Low claudin expression; CD44+/CD24- cancer stem cell markers
- Frequency: ~10%
- Treatment sensitivity: Immunotherapy; CD44-targeting

### 5. IM (Immunomodulatory)
- Profile: High TIL (tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes), immune gene expression, PD-L1 positive
- Frequency: ~15%
- Treatment sensitivity: Best immunotherapy response; pembrolizumab most active
- Key drugs: Pembrolizumab, nivolumab, other immune checkpoints

### 6. LAR (Luminal Androgen Receptor)
- Profile: AR+ (androgen receptor positive), low Ki-67, low immune infiltration
- Frequency: ~15%
- Treatment sensitivity: Lowest pCR to standard chemo; AR-targeted therapy
- Key drugs: Enzalutamide (Enza-T trial), bicalutamide, AR antagonists + CDK4/6 inhibitors

## Tumor-Infiltrating Lymphocytes (TILs)

### Measurement
- % area of stromal TILs in H&E section
- Assessed per International TIL Working Group guidelines

### Clinical Significance in TNBC
| TIL Level | Prognostic Impact | Treatment Response |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| <10% (low) | Worse prognosis | Lower pCR; immunotherapy benefit uncertain |
| 10–59% (intermediate) | Moderate | Intermediate pCR |
| ≥60% (TIL-high) | Best prognosis | Highest pCR rates; pembrolizumab most effective |

- Each 10% increase in TILs → ~2% increase in pCR (meta-analysis)
- High TILs + PD-L1 CPS ≥10 → strongest immunotherapy signal

## Residual Cancer Burden (RCB)

### RCB Score Components
- Primary tumor bed dimensions
- Cellularity in primary bed
- Number of positive nodes
- Size of largest nodal metastasis

### RCB Classes
| Class | Definition | 5-yr DRFS (TNBC) |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| RCB-0 | pCR | ~87% |
| RCB-I | Minimal residual | ~81% |
| RCB-II | Moderate residual | ~58% |
| RCB-III | Extensive residual | ~19% |

- RCB is more granular than binary pCR/non-pCR
- RCB-III: Highest risk → aggressive adjuvant strategy warranted
